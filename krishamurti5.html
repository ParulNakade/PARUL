Biography

Born in 1895 in Madanapalle, India, Krishnamurti was raised in a theosophical community, and for many years, he was seen as the potential leader of the Theosophical Society. However, he rejected this role in 1929, asserting that no one should be the authority on truth.

Throughout his life, Krishnamurti traveled around the world, giving public talks on subjects such as freedom, meditation, relationships, and the nature of the mind. His teachings emphasize the importance of self-awareness, the freedom from conditioned thinking, and the importance of living without conflict.
